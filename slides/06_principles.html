AI Agent Security / Design Principles

Proven Security Principles

These are not new - they made SELinux work for 25 years

1
Boundary Enforcement
Policy engine IN the router, not a sidecar
router/policy.go - Engine embedded in RouterPolicyIntegration
2
Separation of Mechanism & Policy
engine.go (mechanism) + CRDs (policy)
Engine logic separate from AgentPolicy YAML definitions
3
Default Deny
Unknown tools blocked by default
defaultAction: deny in AgentPolicySpec
4
Complete Mediation
Router is the ONLY path to tools
Every tool call goes through Evaluate()
5
Least Privilege
Explicit toolPermissions with constraints
ToolConstraints: pathPatterns, allowedDomains, maxSizeBytes
6
Auditability
Structured JSON logging of ALL decisions
AuditEvent: timestamp, agent, tool, decision, reason
7
Caching for Performance
AVC-pattern cache: ~1µs hit vs ~100µs eval
cache.go: DecisionCache with TTL
8
Progressive Rollout
permissive → enforcing mode transition
EnforcementMode: permissive | enforcing
Why These Principles?

These principles are not theoretical. They are the exact principles that made SELinux successful in protecting Linux systems for 25 years.

Golden Agent does not invent new security theory - it applies proven security theory to a new domain.

Compliance Mapping

Insight

These principles work. They have worked for kernels, VMs, containers, and Kubernetes. They will work for AI agents.
